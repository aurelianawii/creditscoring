SeriousDlqin2yrs is thus the output to be assessed and the rest are inputs for the credit score modelling. For the purpose of this activity, Y/N is then translated to 1/0. A value of closer to 1 indicates more probability of default, whereas value of closer to 0 implies less probability of default.

The pictures above are for reference purpose only, which may help to explain the datasets as below:

  • Payment History: may refer to variables NumberOfTime30-59DaysPastDueNotWorse, NumberOfTimes90DaysLate, and NumberOfTime60-89PastDueNotWorse. No derogatory public record serves the highest score. In case there is one, shorter time since the last record gets lower score and reverse is also true for the opposite.
  • Outstanding Debt: may be related to RevolvingUtilizationOfUnsecuredLines and DebtRatio. A healthy credit with the highest score implies a medium amount of borrowing - not too high or too low.
  • Credit History Length: no variables in the dataset corresponding to this item.
  • Pursuit of New Credit: no variables in the dataset corresponding to this item.
  • Types of credit used / Credit Mix: may correspond to NumberOfOpenCreditLinesAndLoans and NumberRealEstateLoansOrLines. Like Outstanding Debt, a medium number of this item obtains the highest credit score.

FICO also states that they do not take Age, MonthlyIncome, and NumberOfDependents into consideration. However, they will still be included in the model with the following potential relationship:

  • Age: older person of the working age might mean higher credit score as a result of more well-established financial situation.
  • MonthlyIncome: the more the income, the more the ability to meet obligations and thus the higher score.
  • NumberOfDependents: medium number of dependents may be assumed to imply higher score due to the existence of but not excessive number of credit taken.

#Do modifications (cleaning) for Test Data as well
def cleaned_dataset(dataset):
    #age
    dataset.loc[dataset["age"] == 0, "age"] = dataset.age.median()
    
    #monthly income
    age_working = dataset.loc[(dataset["age"] >= 18) & (dataset["age"] < 60)]
    age_senior = dataset.loc[(dataset["age"] >= 60)]
    dataset["MonthlyIncome"] = dataset["MonthlyIncome"].fillna(99999) #to avoid TypeError
    dataset.loc[((dataset["age"] >= 18) & (dataset["age"] < 60)) & (dataset["MonthlyIncome"] == 99999), "MonthlyIncome"] = age_working.MonthlyIncome.mean()
    dataset.loc[(dataset["age"] >= 60) & (dataset["MonthlyIncome"] == 99999), "MonthlyIncome"] = age_senior.MonthlyIncome.mean()
    
    #number of dependents
    dataset["NumberOfDependents"] = dataset["NumberOfDependents"].fillna(0)
    dataset["NumberOfDependents"] = dataset["NumberOfDependents"].astype('int64')
    
    #combined late payments
    dataset["CombinedLatePayment"] = dataset["NumberOfTime30-59DaysPastDueNotWorse"] + dataset["NumberOfTimes90DaysLate"] + dataset["NumberOfTime60-89DaysPastDueNotWorse"] 
    dataset.loc[(dataset["CombinedLatePayment"] >= 1), "CombinedLatePayment"] = 1
    
    #combined loans
    dataset["CombinedLoans"] = dataset["NumberOfOpenCreditLinesAndLoans"] + dataset["NumberRealEstateLoansOrLines"]
    dataset.loc[(dataset["CombinedLoans"] <= 5), "CombinedLoans"] = 0
    dataset.loc[(dataset["CombinedLoans"] > 5), "CombinedLoans"] = 1
    
    #dropping the old features
    dataset.drop(["NumberOfTime30-59DaysPastDueNotWorse", "NumberOfTimes90DaysLate", "NumberOfTime60-89DaysPastDueNotWorse", "NumberOfOpenCreditLinesAndLoans","NumberRealEstateLoansOrLines"], axis = 1, inplace = True)

Choice of Predictive Model ¶

Imbalanced data generate models that are inclined to the dominant class of the output. Since data provided are highly imbalanced (only 6.68% event rate for training data), ensemble models such as Random Forest and Gradient Boosting will be used as they can force both Yes and No classes from SeriousDlqin2yrs to be addressed.
